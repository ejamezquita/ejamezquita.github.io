About

I am a recently minted PhD from the Department of Computational Mathematics, Science and Engineering (CMSE) at Michigan State University. I'm interested in understanding plant morphology using topological data analysis (TDA). I worked under Dr. Dan Chitwood and Dr. Liz Munch. I did my math degree at Universidad de Guanajuato with extensive support from the Mathematics Research Center (CIMAT). Previously, I tried to quantify the morphology of pre-Columbian masks with TDA, and how this might reveal their culture of origin. This archaeological project constituted my bachelor thesis under the guidance of Dr. Antonio Rieser.

Starting Summer 2023 I will be a PFFFD Postdoc Fellow based at the Division of Plant Science and Technology of the University of Missouri.

Research interests: Topological Data Analysis (TDA), Euler Characteristic Transform, X-ray CT scans, image processing, plant morphology.
