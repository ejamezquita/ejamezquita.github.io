1.2 Relating columns to figures

Let's look again at Figure 2 and let's relate it to the data:

For Figure 2a:

  • The Y-axis is "Root weight" per plant, so it is root_dw divided by seed_num (number of seedlings).
  • root_dw is in grams. We'll have to multiply it by 1000 to get miligrams instead.
  • The point scatters are split into Conservational or Traditional soil management practices, encoded as CC or TR, respectively, in the treatment column.
  • They are also differentiated into Bt-expressing transgenic corn line or not, encoded as BT or ISO, respectively, in the corn_trait column.
  • Notice that the plot does not split data into Bt-susceptible or -resistant WCR larvae.

For Figure 2b:

  • The Y-axis is the logarithm of the ratio of root weight of "fed (worm eaten/munched) roots" to "unfed (not eaten) roots".
  • The "fed roots" are those from experiments involving larvae, either SUS or RES.
  • The "unfed roots" are those from CTRL control experiments involving no larvae.

fs = 12
color = ['tab:olive', 'dodgerblue', 'tab:olive', 'dodgerblue']

treat = ['CC','TR','CC','TR']
xvals = [0,1,3,4]

for j in range(len(xvals)):
    df = fit[ (fit['treatment'] == treat[j]) & (fit['corn_trait'] == trait[j]) & (fit['colony'] != 'CTRL') ]
    rw = 1000*(df['root_dw']/df['seed_num'])
    avg, sem = rw.mean(), rw.sem()
    ax.scatter(eps[:len(rw)]+xvals[j], rw, color=color[j], alpha=0.35, ec='k', zorder=1)
    ax.plot( 2*[xvals[j]], [avg - sem, avg+sem], lw=3, c=color[j], marker='_', ms=20)
    ax.scatter(xvals[j], avg, color=color[j], marker='s', s=80, edgecolor='k', zorder=3, label=j)
